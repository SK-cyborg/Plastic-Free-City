Problems of Plastic Pollution in Cities

01. How Big Is the Plastic Problem?

According to the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development's (OECD) Global Plastics Outlook, the world generates approximately 353 million metric tons of plastic waste per year — or almost a million metric tons every day.

Of this, OECD found that only around 9% is recycled. Fifty percent ends up in landfills, and 19% is incinerated. The rest (22%) ends up in dumpsites, leaks into the environment, or is unaccounted for.

02. Where Does Waste End Up?

Much of the environmental plastic enters freshwater and marine ecosystems. Ocean currents carry it long distances to high seas. The notorious Great Pacific Garbage Patch now spans roughly 1.6 million square kilometers.

concerningly, plastic consumption and waste are expected to triple by 2060 compared to 2019 levels.

Plastics pose wide-ranging hazards for people, ecosystems, and the climate:
